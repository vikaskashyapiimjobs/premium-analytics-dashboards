Executive Summary — Key Findings

📉
Feb 2026 saw a modest YoY decline in premium applications (-7.8%) despite a massive -79.5% cut in acquired jobs. Organic jobs absorbed the demand, proving the premium user base is resilient.
📱
Android is the primary drag, accounting for 55% of the total application decline across both premium and all-applicant segments. Android engagement (apps/user) fell -11.4% YoY.
🖥️
Web (Desktop) shows clear recovery — YoY gap closed from -18% in Week 1 to +3.4% by Week 3-4 for premium applications. Web users are the most resilient segment.
📈
Mobile Web is the breakout performer — premium applications grew +23% YoY in Feb, accelerating to +54% by Week 4. Small base but fastest growing channel.
⚠️
iOS engagement declined most steeply (-13.5% apps per applicant), even though applicant count grew +9.3%. iOS users are browsing but applying less.
✅
Overall: the job cleanup had limited impact on premium users. The -36% job supply reduction only translated to -7.8% fewer applications, confirming that non-salary-matching jobs were low-relevance for this cohort.
