Feb 2026: Acquired Jobs Collapsed –80% Due to Salary Filter

In Feb 2026, jobs not matching salary criteria were stopped. Acquired jobs fell from 124/day to 25/day (–79.5%), and organic jobs also dropped –23% (408 → 314/day). Total jobs fell –36%, yet total applications only fell –7.8% — because each remaining job attracted 43% more applications (29 → 42 appl/job). The job filter dramatically reduced supply while demand stayed resilient.

📌

Jan 2026 Was Normal — No Job Disruption

Jan 2026 had nearly the same job count as Jan 2025 (490 vs 502, –2.4%), and acquired jobs actually increased +16%. Applications fell –9.1% purely due to lower applicant traffic, not job supply. The salary filter impact is uniquely a Feb 2026 phenomenon.

Key Insight: Acquired Jobs Had Low Application Yield

Losing ~98 acquired jobs/day would, at the average rate, wipe out ~2,800 applications. But total applications only fell 1,191. This means organic jobs absorbed much of the demand — generating 45 applications per organic job in Feb 2026 vs 38 in Feb 2025 (+20%). Acquired jobs were likely lower-quality listings that weren't driving proportionate engagement.
